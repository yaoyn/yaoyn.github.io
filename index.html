Biography

I am currently a Postdoctoral Fellow, supervised by Dr. Ilenia Battiato, in the Department of Energy Resources Engineering at Stanford University. My current research focuses on using a multiscale approach to optimize energy storage in batteries and membrane systems for water filtration.

I obtained my Ph.D. degree in Civil and Environmental Engineering at Stanford University under the supervision of Dr. Craig Criddle and Dr. Oliver Fringer. Concurrently, I completed two master’s degrees in Civil and Environmental Engineering and Computational Mathematical Engineering, both at Stanford University. My Ph.D. research focused on using a simulation-accelerated approach to optimize energy-neutral wastewater treatment processes for a quick transition from lab-scale to commercial-scale. During my Ph.D., I have been substantially involved in both lab-scale and pilot-scale projects. With my unique background, my skillsets include but are not limited to environmental biotechnology, homogenization theory, computational fluid dynamics (CFD), and artificial intelligence.
